FAQ

What is the experiment factory?

We provide open source behavioral experiments for reproducible science. We are currently in beta, and you can sign up to be notified when we offer deployment of experiments from expfactory.org, or use our experiments for your research. If you use the Experiment Factory for your research, please cite us.

What deployments do you provide?

Our experiments can be deployed locally, or to psiturk as a collection "battery" of experiments either from a local server or via a virtual machine. We recommend you read our getting started guide to determine the best option for you.

Where can I preview the experiments?

You can preview current experiments at http://expfactory.github.io/

How can I contribute?

The Experiment Factory is entirely open source, and you can contribute to any component of the infrastructure on Github with pull requests. You can read more about different ways to contribute, including to code, experiments, and our documentation.

How do I get help?

We suggest that you post issues on the Github repo that is associated with the experiment factory component that you need help with. For example, here is the experiment issue board. This will bring the issue to the attention of our entire team.

We are a team of researchers in the Poldrack Lab at Stanford University. We like to build tools, and have a vision for reproducible science.
